## Industry Context

### Industry Definition
Metal rolling and finishing facilities converting primary metals into finished metal products (sheets, plates, bars, rods) through rolling, annealing, and finishing processes.

### Key Characteristics
- **Production Model**: Continuous rollng operations with annealing and finishing
- **Operating Pattern**: 24/7 continuous operations typical
- **Typical Size**: Large facilities (100-500+ employees)
- **Seasonality**: Low to moderate (customer demand)
- **Supply Chain**: Raw metal ingots/slabs, lubricants, coolants
- **Regulatory**: EPA (emissions, water), OSHA, waste handling
- **Equipment**: Hot/cold rolling mills, annealing furnaces, finishing equipment

### Energy Profile
- **Dominant End-Use**: Furnace heating (annealing), mechanical rolling (electrical)
- **Secondary Uses**: Cooling systems, hydraulics, compressed air
- **Operating Pattern**: Equipment-schedule driven, continuous
- **Peak Periods**: High-speed rolling operations
- **Seasonal Variation**: Customer demand seasonality

## Provisional Volatility Framework (Preliminary)

### Expected Structural Volatility Drivers
- Metal ingot/slab availability and quality
- Rolling specification and product mix diversity
- Process complexity (hot vs. cold rolling, annealing)
- Equipment technology and automation level
- Customer demand variability

### Expected Constraint Volatility Drivers
- Raw material sourcing constraints
- Rolling mill capacity and bottlenecks
- Annealing furnace and cooling infrastructure
- Cooling water and utility capacity
- Skilled mill operator and technician availability

### Expected Performance Volatility Drivers
- Furnace heating energy consistency
- Rolling efficiency and yield
- Annealing temperature consistency
- Equipment utilization and downtime
- Demand forecasting and scheduling accuracy
