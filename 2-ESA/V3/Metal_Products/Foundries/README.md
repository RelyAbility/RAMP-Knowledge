## Industry Context

### Industry Definition
Foundry manufacturing facilities producing cast metal components through melting, molding, casting, and finishing processes from metal ores, scrap, and raw materials.

### Key Characteristics
- **Production Model**: Batch-based casting and finishing
- **Operating Pattern**: Shift-based, often extended hours
- **Typical Size**: Small to large facilities (50-500+ employees)
- **Seasonality**: Moderate (customer demand cycles)
- **Supply Chain**: Metal ores/scrap, mold materials, refractory materials, finishing chemicals
- **Regulatory**: OSHA (safety, noise, dust), environmental (emissions, waste metal)
- **Equipment**: Furnaces, molding equipment, finishing equipment, quality testing

### Energy Profile
- **Dominant End-Use**: Melting furnaces (thermal energy intensive)
- **Secondary Uses**: Compressed air, cooling, material handling
- **Operating Pattern**: Furnace-schedule driven
- **Peak Periods**: High-production casting runs
- **Seasonal Variation**: Customer demand seasonality

## Provisional Volatility Framework (Preliminary)

### Expected Structural Volatility Drivers
- Metal scrap and ore market volatility
- Casting complexity and specification diversity
- Process variability (melting, cooling, finishing)
- Technology and equipment age factors
- Customer order size and timing variability

### Expected Constraint Volatility Drivers
- Metal material sourcing and market volatility
- Furnace capacity and thermal management constraints
- Skilled foundry operator availability
- Environmental/emission control requirements
- Mold and refractory material supply

### Expected Performance Volatility Drivers
- Furnace energy consumption variability
- Defect and rework rate variation
- Casting yield optimization challenges
- Equipment downtime and maintenance impact
- Production scheduling and demand variability
