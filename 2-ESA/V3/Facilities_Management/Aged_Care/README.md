## Industry Context

### Industry Definition
Aged care facilities including nursing homes, assisted living, and long-term care facilities providing residential accommodations and care services for seniors.

### Key Characteristics
- **Facility Type**: Residential care facilities
- **Operating Pattern**: 24/7 operations (resident care continuous)
- **Typical Size**: Medium facilities (100-400 residents, 150-400 staff)
- **Seasonality**: Moderate (climate-dependent)
- **Systems**: HVAC, hot water, electrical, safety systems
- **Regulatory**: Aged Care Quality Standards, building codes, safety
- **Equipment**: Boilers, water heaters, HVAC systems, emergency power

### Energy Profile
- **Dominant End-Use**: HVAC (temperature comfort), hot water
- **Secondary Uses**: Lighting, cooking, laundry, refrigeration
- **Operating Pattern**: Occupancy-driven, continuous
- **Peak Periods**: Cold weather (heating), summer (cooling)
- **Seasonal Variation**: Significant (climate dependent)

## Provisional Volatility Framework (Preliminary)

### Expected Structural Volatility Drivers
- Resident occupancy variability
- Facility age and building condition
- Care service complexity
- Heat and hot water demand from care activities
- Regulatory compliance requirements

### Expected Constraint Volatility Drivers
- Heating/cooling capacity adequacy
- Hot water generation and storage constraints
- Utility infrastructure (metering, capacity)
- Maintenance staff availability
- Building envelope condition (insulation)

### Expected Performance Volatility Drivers
- Occupancy variability (seasonal, admissions)
- Weather-driven HVAC demand
- Hot water demand (bathing, cleaning, cooking)
- Equipment age and reliability
- Facility maintenance prioritization
