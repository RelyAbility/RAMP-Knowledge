## Industry Context

### Industry Definition
Machining and metalworking facilities producing precision metal components and assemblies through turning, milling, grinding, and specialized metalworking processes.

### Key Characteristics
- **Production Model**: Job shop or batch manufacturing
- **Operating Pattern**: Shift-based operations
- **Typical Size**: Small to medium facilities (20-200+ employees)
- **Seasonality**: Low to moderate (customer-driven)
- **Supply Chain**: Metal stock (steel, aluminum, stainless), cutting tools, coolants
- **Regulatory**: OSHA, environmental (coolant disposal, chip recycling)
- **Equipment**: CNC lathes, mills, grinders, specialty machines, quality inspection equipment

### Energy Profile
- **Dominant End-Use**: CNC equipment electrical loads, compressed air (cooling systems)
- **Secondary Uses**: Material handling, lighting, HVAC
- **Operating Pattern**: Equipment-schedule driven
- **Peak Periods**: High-production contract periods
- **Seasonal Variation**: Customer industry cycle dependent

## Provisional Volatility Framework (Preliminary)

### Expected Structural Volatility Drivers
- Customer order complexity and variability
- Precision specification and tolerance requirements
- Metal type and cutting tool complexity
- Equipment technology capabilities (CNC sophistication)
- Market and customer concentration

### Expected Constraint Volatility Drivers
- Metal stock sourcing and availability
- Cutting tool and insert supply
- CNC machine capacity and specialization
- Skilled machinist and programmer availability
- Coolant management and recycling infrastructure

### Expected Performance Volatility Drivers
- CNC equipment utilization and run-time
- Setup/changeover time and efficiency
- Tool wear and replacement impact
- Rework and quality issue rates
- Maintenance and downtime frequency
