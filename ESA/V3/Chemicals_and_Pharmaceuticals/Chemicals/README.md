## Industry Context

### Industry Definition
Chemical manufacturing facilities producing industrial chemicals, specialty chemicals, and chemical intermediates through synthesis, distillation, and chemical processing.

### Key Characteristics
- **Production Model**: Continuous or batch process-based
- **Operating Pattern**: 24/7 continuous operations typical
- **Typical Size**: Large facilities (100-1000+ employees)
- **Seasonality**: Low (customer demand driven)
- **Supply Chain**: Feedstock chemicals, catalysts, solvents, packaging
- **Regulatory**: EPA (emissions, waste), OSHA (safety), transport (hazmat)
- **Equipment**: Reactors, distillation columns, heat exchangers, separation equipment

### Energy Profile
- **Dominant End-Use**: Process heating (steam, thermal), cooling (heat rejection)
- **Secondary Uses**: Pumping, compressed air, electrical drives
- **Operating Pattern**: Process-dependent, often continuous
- **Peak Periods**: Continuous operations
- **Seasonal Variation**: Low (customer demand seasonality minimal)

## Provisional Volatility Framework (Preliminary)

### Expected Structural Volatility Drivers
- Feedstock cost and availability volatility
- Process complexity and reaction kinetics
- Product specification and purity requirements
- Technology and automation level
- Regulatory and environmental stringency

### Expected Constraint Volatility Drivers
- Feedstock sourcing and market volatility
- Reactor capacity and heat transfer constraints
- Cooling and utilities infrastructure
- Skilled process engineer and operator availability
- Environmental compliance infrastructure

### Expected Performance Volatility Drivers
- Process energy consumption consistency
- Yield and purity consistency
- Equipment reliability and downtime patterns
- Temperature and pressure control precision
- Heating/cooling load variability
